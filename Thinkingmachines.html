The piece Fallen Angel by French artist Alexandre Cabanel. I chose this piece primarily for the subject's eyes, which convey a remarkable depth of passion and emotion. While the painting is undeniably beautiful, it’s the complexity of feelings reflected in those eyes that truly captivates me. The eyes don’t just express a single emotion but instead hold a range of feelings, creating a rich narrative. I admire every aspect of this artwork, from the intense lighting that enhances the mood to the vibrant colors and dynamic brush strokes that bring it to life. Together, these elements create a compelling experience that resonates deeply with me, making it a standout piece.

This recreation was remade by Francis Bacon .The original portrait was supposed to evoke a sense of authority but Bacon created it in a way to express the duality of religious figures. Final thoughts

 At least in AI programs like chat GPT for all the paintings that I asked for the cide version for, it said that it would be hard to replicate. Specifically, AI would stay "it relies heavily on expressive, abstract forms, distortion, and emotive color work" or stuff like "intricate detail, atmospheric lighting, and complex landscape" and would give the best version they can even without having the abilities to do so. In the goal of creating a paint that emulates the same emotion and feelings that the original paints give off it was a failure. One thing that I think I learned in doing this project is that AI art can't emulate the experiences and the emotions that the human experience creates. A lot of beautiful art is based on shared experiences and AI art is dead set on making absolute perfection something that is unattainable to humans. There are circumstances where AI art makes something and it's beautiful and perfect but it's always just missing something that you can put finger on. Well That's the thing, it's too perfect, the thing about human experience is that it's messy and people have to work for years to get to a certain point of artistry and complexities but even then they fail and make messy mistakes. Something that AI art just can't properly replicate. But so far in the project I have gained more respect for AI art and just AI in general.
